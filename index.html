Real Time Transfer Learning fro Any-to-Any Voice Conversion

Miao He, Yanghao Zhou, Mingyang Zhang, Heyan Huang, Xian-Ling Mao

Abstract

In recent years, streaming voice conversion (VC) has attracted widespread attention because its real-time performance is more suitable for practical applications than non-streaming VC. Most of the existing streaming VC methods are designed for one-to-one or many-to-many scenarios, while such scenarios are too restrictive and out of touch with reality, so it is important to study any-to-any streaming VC. However, as far as we know, there is currently no research on streaming VC in any-to-any scenarios. Thus, to fill this gap, in this paper, we propose a novel model called Real-Time Transfer Learning Voice Conversion (RTTL-VC), which is based on a non-autoregressive text-to-speech (TTS) system and implemented through transfer learning. Specifically, the proposed method consists of three parts: a speech synthesizer, a voice converter and a streaming processor. The speech synthesizer is first trained for extracting TTS knowledge (the context vectors). Then, in order to better extract the speaker-independent content information, the learned TTS knowledge is transferred to the autoencoder voice converter as the content encoder supervision target. Finally, in streaming processor, the input speeches are divided into chunks to simulate the continuous input flow in real time scenarios and then each chunk is converted sequentially to accomplish streaming inference. Moreover, in mean opinion score (MOS) tests, we observe that the naturalness evaluation metrics cannot reflect the fluency of synthetic speech in streaming scenarios. To address this issue, we propose a new evaluation metric for streaming VC, called fluency. Extensive experiments demonstrate that the proposed method achieves exceptional performance in terms of both latency and conversion quality, making it well-suited for real-time applications. Notably, the method also mitigates the issues of timbre leakage and feature damage commonly encountered in streaming VC. Audio samples are available at https://heixiaoniu.github.io/rttlvc/.

Comparative study of stream and non-stream inference

In order to explore the impact of causal and non-causal structures on our streaming VC, we set the following four combinations according to the causality of encoder and decoder, the number of training steps and the training process are the same:

  • Non-causal-VC(NEND-VC): non-causal encoder and non-causal decoder VC based on FastSpeech2.
  • Non-causal-causal-VC(NECD-VC): non-causal encoder and causal decoder VC based on FastSpeech2.
  • Causal-VC(CECD-VC): causal encoder and causal decoder VC based on FastSpeech2.
  • Causal-non-causal-VC(CEND-VC): causal encoder and non-causal decoder VC based on FastSpeech2.
